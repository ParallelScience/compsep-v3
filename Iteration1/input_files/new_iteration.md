- Decision: PIVOT
- Justification: The current project has reached a definitive "scientific dead-end" regarding the use of global linear estimators (Wiener Filtering) for this specific dataset. The analysis clearly demonstrates that the extreme dynamic range and the non-Gaussian, sparse nature of the tSZ signal render global harmonic-space filters mathematically and numerically inappropriate. Attempting to "fix" the MWF with further regularization or complex bias-correction tables is an exercise in diminishing returns; the fundamental mismatch between the estimator (Gaussian/global) and the signal (sparse/local) will persist. The "Opportunity Cost" of continuing to refine a failed linear pipeline is high. A "Fresh Idea" focusing on the recommended object detection (CNN/U-Net) or local Matched Filter (MF) approaches—which are standard in the field for a reason—would provide a significantly higher "Publishability Delta" and scientific impact. The current results have already provided a valuable negative result that can be summarized in a brief methodological note, allowing the research energy to be redirected toward a more robust, non-linear architecture that aligns with the dataset's physical properties.
