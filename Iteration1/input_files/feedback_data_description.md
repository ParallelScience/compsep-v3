The failure of the analysis is directly attributable to the "Dynamic range warning" described in the dataset description. The description explicitly warns that the tSZ signal is $10^{15}$ times weaker in variance than the CIB at 857 GHz and $10^7$ times weaker than the CMB. It explicitly states: "Never use a global L1/MSE loss on raw tSZ maps" and "Always standardize the tSZ target maps (zero mean, unit variance) before training." 

The research plan and execution failed to implement these constraints, attempting to perform linear algebraic operations (Wiener filtering) on raw, unstandardized maps. This resulted in the "catastrophic numerical collapse" and "numerical suppression" of the signal described in the results, as the extreme dynamic range caused the covariance matrix inversion to become numerically unstable and effectively nullify the signal. The conclusions regarding the failure of the MWF to detect clusters are therefore a direct consequence of ignoring the dataset's explicit warnings regarding dynamic range and the necessity of standardization.
