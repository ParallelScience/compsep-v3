**Title**: Cross-Spectral Wiener Filtering for Optimal tSZ Extraction and Cluster Detection

**Description**: This research objective implements a Multi-Frequency Wiener Filter (MWF) to reconstruct the tSZ Compton-y signal by incorporating the full multi-frequency signal and noise covariance structure, including the CIB cross-power spectra. Unlike hard-nulling constraints, the MWF statistically weights the 90–857 GHz channels to balance CIB suppression against SO/Planck noise amplification, utilizing the `utils.powers` estimator to generate the required cross-frequency power spectra ($\ell \leq 2000$). To handle the 10¹⁵ variance disparity, the signal covariance matrix is regularized by incorporating the CIB auto- and cross-spectra as distinct components, effectively treating the CIB as a correlated foreground rather than a deterministic template. Cluster profiles are modeled using a beam-convolved, frequency-specific profile derived from the FLAMINGO simulation to ensure spatial fidelity at the 1 arcmin target resolution. Detection performance is evaluated through a matched-filter peak-finding algorithm applied to the reconstructed y-map, with completeness and purity quantified against the ground-truth `tsz.npy` catalog as a function of halo mass and local CIB intensity. This approach avoids the instability of rigid projection by providing a statistically optimal estimator that minimizes the mean-square error in the presence of non-Gaussian foregrounds.
